Quick verdict

Best for: guided rollover support

If you want guidance through rollover steps, paperwork, and storage/custodian setup, a specialist-led process can be a fit.

Not ideal for: DIY-first investors

If you prefer a self-service brokerage experience and full pricing control, you may prefer a more independent setup.

What matters most: written fee clarity

Precious metals IRAs often involve custodian + storage fees plus pricing spreads. Ask for the full fee schedule and spread examples in writing before funding.

Fit check (before you request info)

Providers often work best when the account size and rollover eligibility align. This quick check helps avoid wasted calls and keeps leads high-quality.

  • Account size: Do you have roughly $50k+ in a qualified retirement account (or around $20k+ available in cash)?
  • If still employed: If your 401(k) is with your current employer, are you 59½+ or otherwise eligible for an in-service rollover?
  • Time horizon: Are you thinking 5+ years rather than short-term moves?
  • Goal: Diversification and inflation hedging, not “quick gains”.

Fee categories to verify (request in writing)

Fees vary based on custodian, storage option, account size, and the exact metals purchased. The safest approach is to request a written schedule and compare providers apples-to-apples.

  • Account setup (if any)
  • Annual custodian/admin fee
  • Annual storage/insurance fee (and whether storage is segregated)
  • Transaction/processing fees (if any)
  • Pricing spreads/markups on the specific metals you choose
  • Buyback policy: how pricing is determined and timelines

What to verify in writing (before funding)

  • Your minimum based on account type and job status
  • All-in annual costs: custodian + storage + any extras
  • Storage type (segregated vs non-segregated) and the price difference
  • Depository used and how fees are billed
  • Spread examples on the exact products you’re purchasing
  • Promotions: whether they affect spreads/pricing
  • Sell/exit process: timelines and how pricing is determined

Tip: ask them to email the fee schedule so you can compare calmly.

Fees checklist (copy/paste)

Copy/paste this into call notes or an email:

  • What is my exact minimum based on my account type and job status?
  • What are the all-in annual fees (custodian + storage) and are they fixed or tiered?
  • Is storage segregated or non-segregated, and what is the cost difference?
  • Which depository is used and what are the storage charges (annual + any extras)?
  • How is the spread quoted on the exact metals I’m buying (and can you show it in writing)?
  • Do promotions impact pricing/spreads, and are there any one-time vs ongoing terms?
  • What are the steps if I want to sell later (fees, timelines, buyback policy details)?

Ask for written confirmation so you can compare providers apples-to-apples.

FAQ

Is Goldco a good fit for beginners?

It can be a fit if you want guidance through the rollover process, paperwork, and storage/custodian setup. If you prefer a DIY brokerage-style experience, a specialist-led process may feel less flexible.

What fees should I expect with a precious metals IRA?

Common fee categories include account setup (sometimes), annual custodian/admin fees, annual storage/insurance fees, and pricing spreads/markups that vary by product and timing. Always request the full fee schedule in writing.

How long does a 401(k) to Gold IRA rollover take?

Timing depends on your plan administrator and paperwork. A straightforward rollover can take a couple of weeks, but delays can happen when forms are incomplete or extra verification is required.

Do I store the metals at home?

Typically, precious metals in an IRA must be held through an approved custodian and stored through an approved arrangement. Ask the provider to explain storage options and costs clearly.

What should I ask before funding an account?

Ask about minimums for your situation, all-in annual fees (custodian + storage), the storage/depository type, how spreads are quoted, and whether promotions affect pricing.
