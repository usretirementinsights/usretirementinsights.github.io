Gold IRA Basics: How It Works

A Gold IRA is a type of self-directed IRA that can hold certain IRS-approved precious metals. This guide explains the basics in plain language: what it is, how rollovers usually work, common fees, and mistakes that can create avoidable taxes or frustration.

What is a Gold IRA?

A Gold IRA is a retirement account (typically a Traditional or Roth IRA) that can hold certain precious metals instead of (or alongside) typical paper assets. It usually involves an IRA custodian and an approved storage arrangement.

How it works (simple overview)

  1. Open a self-directed IRA with a custodian that supports precious metals
  2. Fund the account (rollover, transfer, or contribution depending on your situation)
  3. Select IRS-approved metals that meet purity requirements
  4. Metals are stored through an approved storage arrangement (not at home)

For most people, the cleanest path is a direct rollover/transfer, which helps avoid avoidable taxes or penalties.

Common fees you may see

  • Account setup (sometimes)
  • Custodian/admin fee (ongoing)
  • Storage fee (ongoing)
  • Dealer spread/markup (varies by product and provider)

Fees vary by provider and account size, so it’s worth comparing the fee schedule in writing.

Timeline: how long does it take?

Timelines depend on your plan administrator and paperwork. Many rollovers take a couple of weeks, but delays can happen if forms are incomplete or the plan has extra verification steps.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using an indirect rollover without understanding deadlines and withholding
  • Buying metals that don’t meet required standards
  • Assuming “home storage” is a simple shortcut
  • Not confirming plan eligibility before starting paperwork
  • Skipping the fee schedule details (custodian + storage + spreads)

Next step: compare providers and the process

If you’re exploring a rollover, start with the step-by-step process so you know what questions to ask. Then compare providers based on clarity, fees, and support.
