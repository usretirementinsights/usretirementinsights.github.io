Gold IRA Basics: How It Works

A Gold IRA is a type of self-directed IRA that can hold certain IRS-approved precious metals. This page is your foundation: what it is, how funding usually works, typical fees, timelines, and mistakes to avoid.

What is a Gold IRA?

A Gold IRA is a retirement account (Traditional or Roth) that can hold certain IRS-approved precious metals instead of (or alongside) typical paper assets. In practice, it usually involves: a specialized IRA custodian, a metals dealer, and an approved storage arrangement.

Important: a Gold IRA is still an IRA. Contribution limits and distribution rules apply the same way.

Who a Gold IRA may be for (and who it may not)

May fit if you want

  • Diversification beyond stocks and bonds
  • A physical asset exposure inside a retirement structure
  • A potential hedge mindset (inflation/currency concerns)

May not fit if you need

  • Maximum liquidity and simplicity
  • Income/yield from holdings
  • Low fee drag (fees can add up)

For a deeper risk discussion, see: Is a Gold IRA safe? Risks and trade-offs.

How it works (simple overview)

  1. Open a self-directed IRA with a custodian that supports precious metals
  2. Fund the account (rollover, transfer, or contribution depending on your situation)
  3. Select IRS-approved metals that meet purity requirements
  4. Metals are stored through an approved arrangement (not at home)

Funding options: rollover vs transfer vs contribution

  • 401(k) rollover: common when changing jobs or retiring (plan rules matter)
  • IRA transfer: IRA-to-IRA movement (often simpler operationally)
  • New contribution: subject to annual IRA limits

Common fees you may see

Fees are where most surprises happen. Ask for the fee schedule in writing and compare consistently.

Fee type What it usually covers What to ask
Account setup Initial onboarding, paperwork, account creation Is it one-time? Is it waived? Get it in writing.
Custodian/admin Annual reporting, recordkeeping, account management Flat fee or %? Any transaction charges?
Storage Depository storage (segregated vs non-segregated) Which storage type? What is the annual total?
Spread/markup Dealer pricing embedded in metals purchase How is pricing set? Any buyback policy?

Timeline: how long does it take?

Timelines depend on your plan administrator and paperwork. Many rollovers take a couple of weeks, but delays can happen if forms are incomplete or if a plan has extra verification steps.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using an indirect rollover without understanding deadlines and withholding
  • Buying metals that don’t meet required standards
  • Assuming “home storage” is a simple shortcut
  • Not confirming plan eligibility before starting paperwork
  • Skipping fee schedule details (custodian + storage + spreads)
