FAQ

Quick answers to common questions. Always confirm details with your custodian/provider.

How does a Gold IRA work?

A Gold IRA is a self-directed retirement account that can hold certain IRS-approved precious metals. Usually you open an account with a custodian, fund it via transfer or rollover, select approved metals, then store them with an approved depository.

What fees should I expect with a Gold IRA?

Typical fees include an account setup fee, annual custodial/administration fees, and storage/insurance fees. Some providers also have markups/spreads on metals. Ask for the full fee schedule in writing.

Can I roll over a 401(k) into a Gold IRA?

Often, yes. Rollovers commonly move funds from a former employer 401(k) into a self-directed IRA. Plan rules vary (especially if you still work for the employer), so confirm your specific plan’s policy.

Where is Gold IRA gold stored?

Gold in an IRA is generally stored at an IRS-approved depository, not at home. Ask which depository is used and what storage options are available.

How do buybacks work if I want to sell later?

Many providers offer a buyback program, but terms vary. Ask how pricing is determined, whether minimums apply, and what fees/spreads may impact the sale.
