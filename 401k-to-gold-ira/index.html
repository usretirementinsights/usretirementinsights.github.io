401(k) to Gold IRA Rollover: How It Works

A 401(k) to Gold IRA rollover allows retirement savers to move part of a traditional 401(k) into a self-directed IRA holding physical precious metals. This guide explains eligibility, steps, costs, risks, and common mistakes.

Disclosure: Informational only. Not financial or tax advice.

Eligibility checklist

  • Former employer 401(k): often eligible
  • Current employer 401(k): depends on plan rules
  • Some plans allow in-service rollovers after age 59½
  • Always confirm with the plan administrator

Learn the basics first: What is a Gold IRA?

Step-by-step rollover process

  1. Confirm eligibility
  2. Open a self-directed Gold IRA
  3. Choose direct rollover if available
  4. Transfer funds custodian-to-custodian
  5. Select IRS-approved metals
  6. Store metals in an approved depository

Fees and costs to understand

  • Account setup fees
  • Annual custodian administration fees
  • Storage fees
  • Dealer pricing spreads

Pros and cons

Pros

  • Diversification
  • Inflation hedge potential
  • Physical asset exposure

Cons

  • Fees
  • No yield
  • Liquidity considerations

Common mistakes

  • Skipping eligibility confirmation
  • Using indirect rollovers without understanding deadlines
  • Ignoring total fees
  • Over-allocating retirement funds
