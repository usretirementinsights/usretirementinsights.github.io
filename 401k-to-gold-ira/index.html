401(k) to Gold IRA Rollover: How It Works

Many retirement savers ask whether they can move part of a traditional 401(k) into a Gold IRA. This guide explains how a 401(k) to Gold IRA rollover works, the rules involved, and what to consider before making a decision.

Can you roll a 401(k) into a Gold IRA?

In many cases, yes. A 401(k) rollover to a Gold IRA may be possible depending on your employment status and the rules of your current plan.

Most rollovers happen after leaving an employer, retiring, or switching jobs. Some plans allow in-service rollovers, but it depends on the plan administrator.

How a 401(k) to Gold IRA rollover works

  1. Confirm rollover eligibility with your plan administrator
  2. Open a self-directed Gold IRA with an approved custodian
  3. Transfer funds from the 401(k) to the new IRA (direct rollover preferred)
  4. Select IRS-approved precious metals for the account

A direct rollover is generally preferred because it helps reduce the risk of taxes or penalties caused by timing mistakes.

Gold IRA rollover rules to know

  • Metals must meet IRS purity standards
  • Assets must be stored with an approved custodian/depository
  • Indirect rollovers can trigger taxes if not completed correctly
  • Standard IRA contribution and distribution rules still apply

Pros and cons of moving a 401(k) into gold

Potential benefits

  • Diversification beyond paper assets
  • Possible hedge against inflation and currency risk
  • Physical asset backing retirement savings

Possible drawbacks

  • Custodian and storage fees
  • Lower liquidity than many traditional holdings
  • May not fit every timeline or risk profile
