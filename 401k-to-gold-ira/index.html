401(k) to Gold IRA Rollover: How It Works

A practical walkthrough: eligibility, steps, timelines, fees to confirm in writing, and mistakes to avoid.

Rollover in 60 seconds

  • A rollover moves retirement funds from a 401(k) into an IRA structure.
  • For precious metals, that IRA is typically a self-directed IRA with a custodian and approved storage.
  • When possible, a direct rollover (plan-to-custodian) reduces timing and withholding surprises.

New to the topic? Start here: Gold IRA basics.

Eligibility checklist (confirm first)

  • Former employer 401(k): often eligible for a rollover
  • Current employer 401(k): depends on plan rules (many restrict rollovers while employed)
  • In-service rollovers: some plans allow after age 59½ (plan-dependent)
  • Always confirm: ask the plan administrator for the rollover rules in writing

Step-by-step rollover process

  1. Confirm eligibility with your plan administrator
  2. Open a Gold IRA with a custodian that supports precious metals
  3. Choose direct rollover if available (plan-to-custodian transfer)
  4. Move funds using the plan’s rollover instructions
  5. Select IRS-eligible metals and confirm pricing details
  6. Store metals through an approved depository arrangement

Tip: Keep a folder with your plan’s rollover instructions, fee schedules, and confirmations.

Typical timeline (what to expect)

  • Eligibility confirmation: same day to 1 week
  • Account setup: 1 to 5 business days (varies by custodian)
  • 401(k) rollover processing: 3 to 15 business days (varies by plan)
  • Metals purchase + storage coordination: varies by provider and inventory

If timelines are vague or constantly shifting, treat it as a signal to slow down and compare.

Fees and costs to understand

  • Custodian/admin fees: account setup + annual administration
  • Storage/insurance fees: depends on storage type (segregated vs commingled)
  • Transaction fees: wires, processing, transfers (if any)
  • Pricing spreads: how the dealer/provider quotes markups on specific metals

Pros and cons (plain-English)

Pros

  • Diversification (different risk drivers)
  • Inflation hedge potential (not guaranteed)
  • Physical asset exposure

Cons

  • Ongoing fees (custodian + storage)
  • No yield (unlike interest/dividends)
  • Liquidity and pricing spreads

For risk details, read: Is a Gold IRA safe?

Common mistakes (avoid these)

  • Skipping eligibility confirmation with your plan administrator
  • Using an indirect rollover without understanding deadlines and withholding
  • Comparing providers without the total fees in writing
  • Assuming storage rules are flexible
  • Over-allocating retirement funds without considering liquidity and risk

Next steps
